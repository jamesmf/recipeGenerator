SOUTHWEST CHICKEN SKILLET


1  c. long-grain rice
4  (20 oz.) chicken breasts, boneless and skinless
1  Tbsp. vegetable oil
20  oz. (2 cans) Tomatoes and Green Chiles*
1/2  c. water
3  green onions, thinly sliced
1  c. Cheddar cheese, shredded
8  oz. (1 tub) sour cream (optional)

      *Ro-Tel brand "Diced in Sauce" Tomatoes and Green Chilies.
      Cook rice according to package directions and set aside.
      Cut chicken breasts into 1-inch chunks.
      Heat oil in large skillet over medium-high heat.  Cook chicken for about 5 minutes, stirring frequently or until no longer pink.
      In a separate pan, combine together:  rice, tomatoes, water and onions (reserve 2 tablespoons for garnish) and bring to a boil.  Cover.  Reduce heat to low.
      Cook for 20 minutes or until rice is tender.  Add chicken to rice mixture and stir together.  Top with cheese and the reserved 2 tablespoon of green onion.  Cover and cook for 5 minutes more, or until cheese melts.
      Serve with sour cream, if desired.
