CHICKEN CHOW MEIN


1  Tbsp. vegetable oil
3  c. slant-sliced celery
1/2  c. slant-sliced green onions with tops
2 1/2  c. hot chicken broth
3  Tbsp. cornstarch
1/8  tsp. garlic powder
1/2  tsp. ground ginger
1/2  c. cold water
1  Tbsp. soy sauce
3 1/4  c. cooked chicken in small pieces
1 (6 oz.)  can mushrooms
1 1/4  c. chopped green pepper
1 (8 oz.)  can water chestnuts, drained and sliced
1 (3 1/2 oz.)  can chow mein noodles
2  Tbsp. finely cut candied ginger

      Heat oil in deep heavy pot; add celery and onions.  Stir-fry over moderate heat until onions are transparent but not brown.  Add chicken broth; cover and cook 4 to 5 minutes.
      Meanwhile, mix cornstarch, salt, garlic, ginger, cold water and soy sauce; mix until smooth.  Add slowly to hot mixture, stirring constantly.  Cook until liquid is thick and clear.  Add chicken, green pepper, mushrooms and water chestnuts; mix well.  Cover and cook on low about 5 minutes or until hot.  Turn into 2-quart serving dish.  Mix noodles and candied ginger and scatter on top of chicken mixture.
