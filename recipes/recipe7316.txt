ROAST TURKEY WITH PAN GRAVY


1  (15 to 18 lb.) turkey (if frozen, thaw in the refrigerator 3 days)
1 1/2  tsp. each salt and pepper, mixed together
1/2  c. (1 stick) unsalted butter, softened
1/2 small  bunch each fresh flat-leaf parsley and fresh thyme sprigs
8  large garlic cloves, smashed
2  fresh or dried California bay leaves, broken in half

 
Giblet Broth:
2 (14 1/2 oz.)  cans reduced-sodium chicken broth plus 2 1/2 c. water
  turkey neck and giblets (no liver)
3  each: shallots, celery stalks and carrots, cut into chunks
  pan gravy

      Place oven rack in lowest position. Preheat oven to 325 degrees;. Remove neck and giblets from turkey; reserve. Rinse turkey; pat dry. Trim fat; sprinkle body and neck cavities with half of salt mixture. With fingers, loosen skin on breast. Spread 6 tablespoons butter under breast skin. Place parsley, thyme, garlic and bay leaves inside body cavity. Tuck wings under; tie legs together. Rub remaining butter over turkey; season with remaining salt mixture.
      Place turkey, breast side up, in large roasting pan. Insert ovenproof meat thermometer into thickest part of thigh, not touching bone. Roast 3 hours for 15 pound turkey, up to 4 1/4 hours for 18 pound, until thermometer registers 170 degrees; (tent with foil if browning too much). Baste with pan juices every 45 minutes, rotating pan each time.
      Transfer bird to board. Tent bird with foil. Let stand 45 minutes. Remove herbs and garlic from bird; reserve herbs and half of garlic for gravy. Pour pan drippings into 2-cup measure; reserve for gravy. Reserve roasting pan as is for making gravy.
      Giblet Broth: In a large saucepan, bring broth, the water, neck and giblets to a boil. Skim foam and discard. Add remaining ingredients. Partially cover; simmer 1 1/2 hours. Strain into bowl; press on solids to release liquids; discard. Add water to equal 4 cups; reserve for making pan gravy. Makes 12 servings with leftovers.
