TACO CHEESECAKE


3  tsp. cornmeal
3  (8 oz.) cream cheese, softened
1  pkg. taco seasoning
1/2  c. sour cream
1/2  c. salsa
2  eggs, lightly beaten
4  oz. shredded pepper Jack cheese
1 (4 oz.)  can chopped green chilies, drained
1/2  c. chopped ripe olives

 
Topping:
8  oz. sour cream
1/4  c. sliced ripe olives
1/4  c. sliced green onions
1/4  c. sliced cherry tomatoes
1  jalapeno pepper, sliced
  tortilla chips or crackers (serve with)

      Sprinkle bottom of greased 9-inch spring-form pan with cornmeal; set aside. In mixing bowl, beat cream cheese until smooth. Add taco seasoning, sour cream and salsa. Stir in eggs, cheese and chilies. Fold in olives. Pour into prepared pan. Place on baking sheet. Bake at 350 degrees; for 30 to 35 minutes or until center is set. Cool on wire rack for 10 minutes. Carefully run a knife around pan to loosen. Cool for 1 hour. (Top of cheesecake will crack.) Refrigerate couple of hours or overnight. Remove sides of pan.
       Spread sour cream over top and sides of cheesecake. Arrange olives, green onions, tomatoes and peppers on top. Serve with tortilla chips or crackers. Makes 18 to 20 servings.
