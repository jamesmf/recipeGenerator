SPICY MEAT BALLS IN SWEET AND SOUR SAUCE


1 1/2  lb. ground beef
1/2  lb. ground pork
1  c. milk
1/2  c. whole wheat bread crumbs
1 1/2  tsp. salt
1/2  tsp. ginger
1/2  tsp. allspice
1/4  tsp. ground white pepper
1  c. minced onions
1  Tbsp. unsalted butter

      Mix beef and pork. Put through food processor with steel blade until meat is smooth. Combine meat, milk, bread crumbs, salt, ginger, allspice and pepper in large bowl. Let stand 5 minutes.
      Saut√© onions in butter; add meat mixture.  Mix thoroughly. Shape into balls about 1 teaspoon each.  Place in jelly roll pan. Bake until lightly brown, 10 to 15 minutes.  Transfer to clean baking pan.  Cool.  Can freeze in airtight container for up to a month.  Heat in sweet-sour pineapple sauce.
Sauce:
2 large  cans pineapple chunks (in natural juice)
1/4  c. cornstarch
1/4  c. brown sugar
1  c. chicken broth
3/4  c. white wine vinegar
1  Tbsp. soy sauce
1  lb. ketchup
1  c. diced onion
1  large green bell pepper, cut in small pieces
1 (8 oz.)  can water chestnuts, drained

      Just before serving time, drain pineapple, reserving 1 1/4 cups juice. Mix cornstarch and brown sugar in large saucepan. Stir in reserved pineapple juice, chicken broth, vinegar, soy sauce and ketchup. Heat over high heat, stirring constantly to boiling. Reduce heat to medium-low. Cook, stirring constantly, until sauce thickens and bubbles for two minutes. Add onion and green pepper. Cook and stir for 1 minute.
      Gently stir frozen meat balls, pineapple chunks and water chestnuts into sauce. Reduce heat to low. Cook covered until meat balls are heated through, about 15 minutes. Keep warm in chafing dish.
