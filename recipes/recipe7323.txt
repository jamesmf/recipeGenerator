COCONUT-MACADAMIA SHRIMP SKEWERS

Plum Sauce:
1  c. bottled Asian plum sauce
1 1/2  tsp. each: bottled sambal oelek (ground chile paste), honey and soy sauce

 
Crumb Coating:
1 1/2  c. panko (Japanese bread crumbs) or coarse white bread crumbs, dried until very crisp
3/4  c. shredded, dried unsweetened coconut
3/4  c. finely chopped Macadamia nuts

 
Shrimp:
  vegetable oil (for frying)
1/4  c. all-purpose flour
24  large shrimp (about 1 1/2 lb.), peeled and deveined, leaving tail shells intact
1/3  c. unsweetened coconut milk
1  large egg
1/2  tsp. each salt and black pepper

 
Garnish (Optional):
  Asian greens or watercress

      You will need 24 (6-inch) bamboo skewers.
      Sauce: In bowl, mix ingredients.
      Coating: In bowl, mix ingredients.
      Shrimp: Heat 1 1/2-inches of oil in deep-fryer or Dutch oven to 350 degrees;.
       Put flour into large plastic food storage bag. Add shrimp, a few at a time; shake to coat. Transfer to waxed paper. Thread skewer through tail and head of each shrimp.
      In bowl, whisk coconut milk and egg. Dip shrimp, one at a time, in coconut-milk mixture, then in crumb coating.
      In hot oil, fry 6 skewers at a time, 3 minutes per batch, turning once, until golden. Drain on paper towel. Sprinkle with salt and pepper. Garnish, if desired. Serve with Plum Sauce. Makes 6 first-course servings.
