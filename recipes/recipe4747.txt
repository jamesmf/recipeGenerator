CORN BREAD AND RAJAS DRESSING

Corn Bread:
  cooking spray
2  c. yellow cornmeal
2/3  c. all-purpose flour
4  tsp. baking powder
1 1/2  tsp. each sugar and salt
1 2/3  c. buttermilk
4  large eggs, beaten
6  Tbsp. unsalted butter, melted

 
Sweet Pepper Rajas:
3  each red and yellow bell peppers, halved lengthwise and seeded
4  poblano chiles, halved lengthwise and seeded
3  Tbsp. olive oil
1  sweet onion, halved and sliced thin
1  tsp. salt
1/2  tsp. black pepper
1/4  c. chopped fresh parsley
1  large egg, beaten
1/2  c. (1 stick) unsalted butter
2 1/2  c. chicken broth
1 1/2  Tbsp. each chopped fresh sage and thyme
1/2  tsp. each salt and black pepper

      CORN BREAD: Preheat oven to 375 degrees;. Line 13 x 9 x 2-inch baking pan with foil; with cooking spray, coat foil. In a large bowl, whisk cornmeal, flour, baking powder, sugar and salt. Whisk in buttermilk and eggs, then melted butter just to blend. Scrape into pan. Bake 30 minutes, until golden. Cool in pan. Cut into 3/4-inch cubes; spread on baking sheet. Dry in 250 degrees; oven 30 minutes.
      RAJAS: Preheat broiler. On foil-lined baking sheet, broil peppers and chiles, cut side down, 20 to 30 minutes, until evenly charred. Transfer to paper bag; seal and cool. Remove skins; cut into strips. Put in large bowl. In nonstick 12-inch skillet, heat oil. Add onion; cook until golden brown, 12 minutes. Add to peppers. Add salt and pepper. Add Corn Bread, parsley and egg.
      Preheat oven to 400 degrees;. Butter shallow 4-quart baking dish. In same skillet as before, melt butter. Add remaining ingredients. Bring to a boil. Add Corn Bread mixture. Put in prepared dish; cover with foil (can be made day ahead; refrigerate). Bake 10 minutes. Uncover. Bake 25 minutes until golden brown. Makes 12 cups.
