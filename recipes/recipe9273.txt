MEXICAN LIME CHICKEN

Marinade for Chicken:
1  c. water
1/3  c. teriyaki sauce
2  Tbsp. lime juice
2  tsp. minced garlic
1/2  tsp. salt
1/4  tsp. pepper
4  chicken breast fillets
1  c. shredded cheddar monterey jack cheese blend

      Mix marinade ingredients in a medium bowl.  Add the chicken and chill for 2 hours. Preheat oven to high broil.  Cut the chicken into strips for faster cooking under broiler; cook until done.  When done, arrange the chicken in a baking pan.  Spread a layer  of Mexi-ranch dressing on each portion of chicken, followed by 1/4 c. of the shredded cheese.  Broil for 2 to 3 minutes, or just until the cheese has melted.  To serve, spread a bed of tortilla strips on each of 4 plates.  Slide a chicken portion onto the chips and serve with your favorite rice or other Mexican dish. Serves 4.
Mexi-Ranch Dressing:
1/4  c. mayonnaise
1/4  c. sour cream
1  Tbsp. milk
2  tsp. minced tomato
1 1/2  tsp. white vinegar
1  tsp. minced canned jalapeno slices (nacho slices)
1  tsp. minced onion
1/4  tsp. dried parsley
1/4  tsp. Tabasco pepper sauce
1/8  tsp. salt
1/8  tsp. dried dill weed
1/8  tsp. paprika
1/8  tsp. cumin
1/8  tsp. chili powder
  dash garlic powder
  dash ground black pepper

      Combine all ingredients in a medium bowl; mix well until smooth.  Cover and chill until needed.
Fried Tortilla Strips:
8  (6 inch) tortillas
2  c. cooking oil

      Cut stack of tortillas in half; slice the tortillas into thin strips.  Fry the tortilla strips in preheated oil in a large skillet for 3 to 5 minutes, or until crispy.  Salt lightly and cool on paper towels to drain.
