APPLE PIE

Filling:
1  c. sugar
2  Tbsp. flour
1/2  tsp. cinnamon
1/4  tsp. nutmeg
1/4  tsp. salt
1  tsp. lemon juice
5  c. thinly sliced apples
2  Tbsp. butter or margarine

      Combine sugar, flour, spices, salt and lemon juice.  Add apples; toss to mix.  Roll out pastry dough for crust and top.  Cut vents in top piece.  Line pie pan with pastry and cut off overhang. Fill with half the apples and dot with butter.  Add remaining apples.  Moisten rim with water.  Adjust top crust.  Leave about 1/2-inch pastry beyond edge.  Fold in under lower pastry.  Seal rim by pressing together and flute it.  Brush with milk.  Bake at 400 degrees; for 50 to 60 minutes or until apples test done with fork.  For a single crust, use a 425 degrees; oven.
Pastry:
2 1/4  c. flour (take out 1/3 c. flour)
4  Tbsp. water
1  tsp. salt
3/4  c. shortening

      Combine 2 1/4 cups (less 1/3 cup) flour and salt.  Cut the shortening into remaining flour until pieces are the size of small peas.  Mix 4 tablespoons of water with the 1/3 cup flour to form a paste.  Add to shortening and flour mixture.  Mix and shape into ball.  Divide dough into 2 parts.
