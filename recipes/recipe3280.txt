REFRIED BEAN NACHOS

Refried Beans:
1  lb. dried pinto beans, soaked according to pkg. directions
2  dried ancho chiles, stems removed, seeded and chopped
8  c. cold water
1/4  c. olive oil
2  slices bacon, finely chopped
2/3  c. minced white onion
4  cloves garlic, minced
1  Tbsp. kosher salt

 
Guacamole:
2  ripe avocados, cut in small cubes
3/4  c. minced white onion
2  serrano chiles, seeded and minced
2 1/2  Tbsp. fresh lime juice
2  Tbsp. chopped fresh cilantro
2  tsp. olive oil
1  tsp. kosher salt
1/4  tsp. black pepper
1  tomato, chopped
4  doz. corn tortilla chips
1/2  lb. Cheddar, shredded
1/2  c. sliced pickled jalapeos

      BEANS: In Dutch oven, bring beans, chiles and the water to a boil. Simmer, partially covered, 1 1/2 hours until very tender. Drain beans, reserving 1 cup liquid. In processor, in batches, puree beans and reserved liquid (can be done up to 3 days ahead; refrigerate).
       In a large skillet, heat oil. Add bacon; cook until crisp. Add onion and garlic; cook until golden brown, 4 minutes. Add beans; cook, stirring, until oil is absorbed, mixture thickened and creamy, 10 to 12 minutes. Add salt. Remove 2 3/4 cups. Refrigerate or freeze for other use. Keep beans in pot warm.
      GUACAMOLE: In bowl, combine avocado, onion, chiles, juice, cilantro, oil, salt and pepper. With spoon, blend ingredients, lightly mashing avocado, leaving small chunks. Stir in tomato (can be made day ahead; refrigerate).
      TO SERVE: Preheat broiler. Arrange chips on 2 large baking sheets. Spoon 1 tablespoon beans on each chip. Sprinkle with cheese. Place jalapeo on each. Broil 1 pan at a time, until cheese melts. Transfer to platter. Top each with guacamole. Serve.
