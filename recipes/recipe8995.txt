CHILI IN BREAD BOWLS


1  Tbsp. all-purpose flour
1/4  tsp. salt
1/8  tsp. pepper
1/2  lb. each: lean beef stew meat, boneless skinless chicken breast and boneless pork, cut into cubes
1  Tbsp. vegetable oil
1  medium onion, chopped
1  medium green pepper, chopped
1  jalapeno pepper, seeded and chopped*
1 (28 oz.)  can diced tomatoes, drained
1 (16 oz.)  can kidney beans, rinsed and drained
1 (15 1/2 oz.)  can navy beans or Great Northern beans, rinsed and drained
1 (8 oz.)  can tomato sauce
1  Tbsp. chili powder
1  garlic clove, minced
1 1/2  tsp. ground cumin
1/2  tsp. dried basil
1/4 to 1/2  tsp. cayenne pepper
9  large hard rolls
  sour cream, chopped green onions and sweet red pepper (optional)

      In a large resealable plastic bag, combine the flour, salt and pepper. Add meat in batches. Toss to coat.
      In a large skillet, brown meat in oil in batches. Transfer to a 5-quart slow cooker with a slotted spoon. Stir in onion, peppers, tomatoes, beans, tomato sauce and seasonings. Cover and cook on low for 7 to 8 hours or until the meat is tender.
       Cut tops off rolls. Carefully hollow out bottom halves. Spoon about 1 cup of chili into each roll. Garnish with sour cream, onions and red pepper, if desired. Yield: 9 servings.
      *Editor's Note: When cutting or seeding hot peppers, use rubber or plastic gloves to protect your hands. Avoid touching your face.
