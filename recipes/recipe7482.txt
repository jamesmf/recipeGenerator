HAM WITH CIDER GLAZE


1  cooked bone-in ham (5 to 6 lb. rump or shank portion)
2  c. apple cider or apple juice
1  c. honey
1/2  c. cider vinegar
1/4  c. Dijon-style mustard
2  tsp. chili powder
1  Tbsp. butter
1/2  tsp. apple pie spice

      Preheat oven to 325 degrees;.  Score ham with diagonal cuts in a diamond pattern.  Place ham on a rack in a shallow roasting pan. Insert a meat thermometer; bake ham until thermometer registers 140 degrees; (about 2 hours).  Meanwhile, in a large saucepan combine cider, honey, vinegar, mustard, and chili powder.  Bring to boiling; reduce heat.  Boil gently, uncovered, about 15 minutes, stirring frequently (watch carefully, as mixture will bubble up). You should have about 2 cups of mixture.  Remove half of the mixture to a small bowl. Stir in butter and 1/4 teaspoon of the pie spice; set aside for sauce.
      Bring remaining mixture in saucepan to boiling; reduce heat. Boil gently for 5 minutes or until mixture is thickened and to reduced to about 1/2 cup; stir frequently.  Stir in remaining 1/4 teaspoon pie spice; use for glaze. Brush ham with glaze the last 20 to 30 minutes of baking. Reheat cider sauce in microwave-safe bowl for 1 minute on high heat or heat in small saucepan.  Pass sauce with ham.  Serves 16 to 20.
