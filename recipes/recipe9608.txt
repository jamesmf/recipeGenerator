ROLLED, STUFFED FLANK STEAK


1 1/2  lb. flank steak
3  Tbsp. butter
1/2  c. chopped celery
2  Tbsp. chopped onion
2 1/2  c. white bread cubes
1  tsp. dried parsley flakes
1/2  tsp. salt
1/4  tsp. poultry seasoning
1/4  tsp. dried marjoram
1/8  tsp. pepper
1 1/2  Tbsp. flour
1 (3 oz.)  can sliced mushrooms (undrained)

      Wipe steak with damp paper towel. Trim excess fat.  Score surface in diamond pattern on both sides 1/8-inch deep.  Melt 2 tablespoons butter in Dutch oven or casserole. Add celery and onion and saut√© until tender.  Remove from heat. Add bread cubes, parsley, salt, poultry seasoning, marjoram and pepper, tossing lightly until well mixed.  Place stuffing lengthwise down center of steak, starting at narrow end.  Roll up steak.  Secure with skewers.
      Start oven to 350 degrees;.  Melt remaining butter in Dutch oven or casserole. Brown steak well on both sides in melted butter.  Spoon mushrooms over top (including their liquid).  Bake, covered, 1 1/2 hours or until steak is tender.  Remove steak to heated platter; keep warm.  Pour mushrooms and liquid from cooking utensil into bowl. Skim off fat. Measure 1 cup liquid and return to utensil. Combine flour and 2 tablespoons of water, stirring to make a smooth paste. Then stir into liquid to combine well.  Bring mixture to boiling.  Reduce heat and simmer 2 minutes.  Slice steak crosswise.  Pour gravy over each serving.
